# Masar Training

**Masar Training** is a field-ready cybersecurity program provided by **NCSC** in collaboration with **SKIDZ**.
It is designed to build practical, job-relevant skills across the three main cybersecurity pillars:

- **Offensive Security**
- **Defensive Security**
- **Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC)**

The program combines structured learning with practical exercises that reflect real-life cybersecurity scenarios, helping learners build both technical depth and operational awareness.

---

## Overview

Masar Training offers a balanced learning path for anyone looking to strengthen their cybersecurity foundation through guided sessions and hands-on practice. The content is organized to cover both technical and strategic areas of security, making it suitable for learners who want a well-rounded understanding of the field.

---

## Program Focus

### Offensive Security
Covers the fundamentals of ethical hacking, reconnaissance, scanning, vulnerability assessment, exploitation, post-exploitation, reporting, command and control, and secure coding.

### Defensive Security
Focuses on blue team concepts, incident handling and response, digital forensics, secure configuration management, threat intelligence, and threat hunting.

### Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC)
Introduces governance models, security frameworks, risk management in practice, and business continuity and resilience.

---

## Learning Approach

The training is built around a practical approach that helps connect theory to real-world application.

- Structured session-based learning
- Coverage of core cybersecurity domains
- Practical exercises aligned with realistic scenarios
- Progressive path from foundational topics to more advanced concepts

---

## Why This Program Stands Out

Masar Training provides a clear and practical roadmap across the most important cybersecurity disciplines. It helps learners build hands-on technical skills while also understanding the governance, compliance, and risk side of security operations.
